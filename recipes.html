Persian Recipes

Gheymeh

Gheymeh stew, also known as Gheimeh or Gheimeh Polo, is a traditional Iranian dish that holds a special place in Persian cuisine. This flavorful stew features diced meat, often lamb or beef, cooked with yellow split peas, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic spices such as turmeric and cinnamon. The dish is typically garnished with fried potatoes, creating a delightful combination of textures. Gheymeh stew is renowned for its rich and savory taste, reflecting the culinary heritage of Iran. It is commonly served with rice, creating a satisfying and hearty meal that is cherished in Iranian households and enjoyed by those seeking a taste of authentic Persian flavors.

Ingredients

  • Onion: 2 large
  • Red meat: 1 lb
  • Cotyledons: 0.5 lb
  • Tomato paste: 2 Tbsp
  • Water: 2 cups
  • Oil: 5 Tbsp
  • Spices: tumeric, black pepper, cinemon, salt

Steps

  1. Chop the onions and fry them in a pot with oil
  2. Add the spices to the pot once onions are fried
  3. Add the red meat to the pot and stir it for 5 minutes
  4. add the Cotyledons and water to the pot and let it boil for 1.5 on meduim heat
  5. fry your tomato paste for 2 minutes with oil and add to the pot

Side Dishes

Gheymeh stew is often served with complementary side dishes to enhance the overall dining experience. some of the traditional side dishes are: Torshi, Shirazi salad, and Yogurt with cucumber

Potato Kookoo

Kookoo Sibzamini is a delectable Iranian dish that translates to "potato kookoo" in English. Kookoo Sibzamini is known for its delightful combination of flavors and textures, offering a crispy crust with a soft and flavorful interior. It is often served as a popular street food in Iran or enjoyed as a side dish during family gatherings. This potato kookoo showcases the versatility and ingenuity of Persian cuisine, making it a beloved dish among those who appreciate the rich culinary heritage of Iran.

Ingredients

  • Onion: 1 Meduim
  • potato: 3 meduim
  • eggs: 3 large
  • Oil: 5 Tbsp
  • Spices: tumeric, black pepper, garlic powder, salt

Steps

  1. shred you onion and squeeze the juice out of it and add the rest to a bowl
  2. shred your potato into the bowl
  3. crack the eggs in the bowl and add all the spices
  4. Mix all the ingredients well.
  5. heat up the pan and add your oil. add the batter to the pan make the surface smooth. put the lid on and let it cook for 10 minutes.
  6. then flip the kotlet and let the other side fried for 7 minutes

Side Dishes

Kookoo Sibzamini pairs well with various side dishes that complement its flavors and textures. some of the side dishes are Torshi, Fresh herbs, Torshi, Yogurt with mint, and Shirazi salad.

Herbs Kookoo

Kookoo Sabzi, a popular Persian herb frittata, stands as a vibrant and aromatic dish celebrated for its blend of fresh herbs and unique flavors. Kookoo Sabzi is often enjoyed as a main dish or served in wedges as part of a mezze-style spread. Its verdant hue and the medley of herbal aromas make it a visually appealing and delightful addition to Persian cuisine, reflecting the rich culinary heritage of Iran.

Ingredients

  • Onion: 1 Meduim
  • chopped herbs: 0.75 lb
  • eggs: 3 large
  • Oil: 5 Tbsp
  • chopped walnuts: 3 Tbsp
  • Spices: tumeric, black pepper, garlic powder, salt

Steps

  1. Add the chopped herbs into a bowl and add the spices and chooped walnuts
  2. crack the eggs inside the bowl and mix everything together
  3. heat up the pan and add your oil. Add the batter to the pan make the surface smooth. Put the lid on and let it cook for 10 minutes.
  4. then flip the kotlet and let the other side fried for 7 minutes

Side Dishes

Kookoo Sabzi pairs well with a variety of side dishes that complement its herbaceous flavors and enhance the overall dining experience. some of the sides are Yogurt with cucumber, Torshi, and Feta cheese.
